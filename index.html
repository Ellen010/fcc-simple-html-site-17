Trading vocabulary

MAE (Maximum Adverse Excursion)

This is one of the most commonly referenced metrics. It represents the largest unfavorable movement a trade experiences after being opened. For instance, if you purchase AAPL at $145 and the price drops to $144 before recovering, your MAE is $1.

MFE (Maximum Favorable Excursion)

The counterpart to MAE, MFE measures the maximum favorable movement a trade achieves while it remains open. Using the AAPL example again, if you buy at $145 and the price rises to $150 before closing the trade at $149, your MFE is $5.

DOM (Depth of Market)

For traders who donâ€™t rely on charts, DOM offers an intuitive view of the market. It displays the prices at which participants are willing to buy and sell, along with the size (quantity) at each price level. Chart traders may find DOM less familiar, but it essentially visualizes the market as a collection of buy and sell orders, showing both sides of the price along with the corresponding order sizes.

Bid

The bid is the price a buyer is willing to pay for an asset. For example, if you want to buy AAPL and are only willing to pay up to $146, you place a limit order at $146, joining others who are also bidding at that price.

Ask (or Offer)

The ask is the opposite of the bid. It indicates the price and quantity at which a seller is willing to sell.

VAL, VAH, VWAP

These terms relate to the position of price within a market profile. VAH (Value Area High) is the highest price within the range where 70% of trading activity occurred. VAL (Value Area Low) is the lowest price within the same range. VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price) provides an average price based on volume and time.
